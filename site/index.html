01 — Philosophy

We believe the bath is the last true refuge — and that it deserves to be carved, not cast. Stone remembers water.

— The Atelier, Earth Made

02 — The Collection

Six stones.
Six vessels.

Each piece is a single block of one material, carved to order. We choose the stone for the room, never the room for the stone.

No. 001

The Monolith

Honey Travertine

The piece that began the atelier. A single block of honey travertine, hollowed by hand until the grain itself decides where the water gathers — warm to the touch even before it is filled.

Beverly Hills · Joshua Tree · Miami Request this piece
No. 002

Lumen

Illuminated Onyx

Onyx is the only stone that holds light. We line each Lumen with a warm source and the vessel glows from within — a lantern you bathe inside. After dark, against a skyline, it becomes the room.

Tokyo · New York · Bali Request this piece
No. 003

Erebus

Black River Stone

A boulder lifted whole from a riverbed — its outer skin left raw, its basin honed to black glass. The contrast of wild and worked. The heaviest vessel we make, and the most quiet.

The Garden House Request this piece
No. 004

Rosa

Rosa Marble

Blush veins drawn through warm stone, polished to a soft sheen. Rosa takes the colour of every sunrise and sunset it faces, and is never quite the same hour twice.

No. 005

Aurora

Raw Rose Quartz

The rarest vessel we carve. A monolith of rose quartz, left in its crystalline rough on the outside and smoothed only where the body rests. Light enters the stone and scatters pink.

Calacatta

White Calacatta Marble

Bright white marble threaded with grey, carved into a thin, sweeping bowl. The most architectural vessel of the collection — it belongs to glass houses and high, clean light.

03 — Lumen

Light,
made liquid.

Our signature vessel is carved from a single shell of honey onyx thin enough to pass light. Lit from beneath, it turns water to amber and the darkest room to a glow. By night, above a skyline, Lumen is no longer furniture — it is the source.

04 — Craft

From quarry
to threshold.

01

The block is read

A single mass is chosen at the quarry. Its veins decide where the water will fall.

02

Hollowed by hand

Months of carving remove four hundred kilos, never adding a seam. The form emerges; it is not assembled.

03

Honed & sealed

The surface is brought to a soft, hand-warm finish and sealed to hold a century of water.
